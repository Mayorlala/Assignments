Nelson Mandela

First Black President of South Africa

Here is a timeline of Nelson Mandela life:

  • 1918 - born in Mvezo, South Africa
  • 1944 - He joined the African National Congress (ANC), a Black-liberation group, and became a leader of its Youth League
  • 1944 - He married Evelyn Ntoko Mase
  • 1952 - with fellow ANC leader Oliver Tambo, Mandela established South Africa’s first Black law practice, specializing in cases resulting from the post-1948 apartheid legislation
  • 1955 - He was involved in drafting the Freedom Charter, a document calling for nonracial social democracy in South Africa
  • 1956 - He was arrested with more than 100 other people on charges of treason that were designed to harass antiapartheid activists
  • 1961 - He was acquitted
  • 1961 - During the extended court proceedings, he divorced his first wife and married Nomzamo Winifred Madikizela
  • 1993 - He and De Klerk were jointly awarded the Nobel prize for peace
  • 1994 - He became the first Black president of South Africa
  • 2013 - He died at the age of 95 years
